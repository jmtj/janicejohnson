Portfolio Website

As a seasoned professional, I wanted a place to showcase my broad range of work and detailed cases studies. This site is intended to help viewers get a deeper understanding of my work and who I am as a solution designer and a person.

Overview
Project

Developing my own web-site, enabled me to update my previous knowledge of HTML and develop an understanding of CSS and JavaScript to better understand what is required from designers to set up developers for the best success to implement designs. It also gave me a sense of pride to own the site from the start and to evolve it as my story and skills evolve.

Challenges

With a background in email development and basic HTML, I had to relearn the more recent HTML best practices. My CSS experience was minimal, and my JavaScript experience was zero. I learned from a course through Career Foundry and on-line searching. W3Schools is a resource I found very valuable.

Two of my natural talents are being resourceful and making processes more efficient. Those skills lean towards the discovery through ideation phases of design. It is easy for me to know what looks good when I see it and this project helped me strengthen pulling the typography and colors together from scratch.

My Role
  • Sole Web Developer and UX designer for the entire project
  • Completed as part of Career Foundry UX Design certification requirement
My Approach and Tools
  • Responsive Design approach which allows the layout to adapt to the size of the device utilized by each user.
  • Figma for wireframing
  • Visual Studio for coding
  • GitHub for sharing, versions and collaboration

Discover

To help me decide on the content, features, and layout I wanted to use for my portfolio site, I explored examples of other designers' portfolios.

I was drawn to the sites that used neutral colors and white space. I felt this was the best way to showcase the work I have done.

Ideate

I started by gathering and organizing my content in a centralized location to determine what I wanted to share. Then I built upon the wireframe templates that the course provided, adding personalization that made sense for my content.
