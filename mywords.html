My Words

My Words was designed to:

  • help young adults learn new terminology
  • enable them to enter their own terms to learn and group them by subjects of their choosing
  • give a variety of methods to practice to help keep them engaged
  • make learrning easy and accessible users are encouraged by the app to learn their terms

I was the only UX Designer for this project that was completed as part of Career Foundry UX Design certification requirement.

Competitive Analysis

For this analysis I focused on ease of use and scope of capabilities. They all met the general criteria of creating flashcards to learn terminology. They all had the ability to create custom terms and answers. They each had some extras that would meet additional needs depending on the users’ need for the products.

User Interviews

DOING | THINKING | FEELING

Who participated:
  • 5 participants
  • Age Range: 22-37 years
  • Students and Professionals
Findings
  • All interviewees felt repetition and application to real life was important for learning and retention. If they do not apply what they learn, they will forget in a matter of weeks or months.
  • Competition motivates some people but not everyone – it can turn some people off or add pressure they do not want.
  • Reminder notifications are valuable to keep the learner engaged but should have the ability to set to learner’s preferences.
  • Variety is the spice to learning. All interviewees felt more engaged with terms and information when they had a variety of ways to interact with the information.
  • The interviewees feel more engaged with the learnings when supporting information is provided – what is the backstory of why things are – rather than a list of facts or terms.
  • Most expressed that the experience needs to be pleasing. They referenced good visuals, organization, navigation and variety as part of a good experience. If the app does not provide these, they won’t use it. “There are too manyoptions out there if this does not work, I am moving on – time is too precious – you will gravitate to what is easier and pleasing.” ~Mari
  • Imposture syndrome is common, and people want ways to expand their knowledge in their fields to be productive and valuable.
